Peer Mentorship Program

Connect with experienced students to enhance your university experience, get advice, and navigate academic and social life.

Welcome to the Peer Mentorship Program

The Peer Mentorship Program at the University of Wolverhampton is designed to help new students adjust to university life by pairing them with experienced students who can offer guidance and support. Whether you're a first-year student or a returning student looking for advice, our mentors are here to help you navigate both academic and social aspects of university life.

What is Peer Mentorship?

Peer mentorship is a program where upper-year students, who have been trained to offer support, guide newer students through their university journey. This program creates a supportive network for students, helping them feel more confident and less isolated during their academic experience.

Benefits of Peer Mentorship:

  • Personalized Guidance: Get advice tailored to your academic and personal experiences.
  • Academic Support: Receive tips on studying, time management, and academic resources.
  • Social Integration: Build connections and feel more comfortable within the university community.
  • Career Advice: Gain insights from experienced students on career opportunities and internships.
  • Emotional Support: Have someone to talk to who understands the challenges of university life.

How the Peer Mentorship Program Works

The Peer Mentorship Program is designed to be flexible and accessible to all students. Here's how it works:

  1. Sign Up: New students can sign up for the Peer Mentorship Program at the beginning of each semester. Upper-year students interested in becoming mentors will also sign up.
  2. Pairing: Once you sign up, you'll be matched with a mentor based on your course, interests, and personal preferences.
  3. Regular Meetings: Mentors and mentees will meet regularly—whether in person or virtually—to discuss academic questions, university life, and general well-being.
  4. Support and Advice: Mentors will provide ongoing support, share resources, and offer advice based on their own university experiences.
  5. Evaluation: At the end of the semester, both mentors and mentees will provide feedback on the program to ensure continuous improvement.

Becoming a Mentor

If you're an experienced student who wants to give back to the university community, becoming a mentor can be a rewarding experience. As a mentor, you’ll have the opportunity to:

  • Develop Leadership Skills: Mentoring is a great way to improve your communication, leadership, and interpersonal skills.
  • Make a Difference: Help new students navigate university life and become part of their support system.
  • Enhance Your CV: Being a mentor looks great on your CV and shows employers you are committed to supporting others and developing leadership skills.
  • Expand Your Network: Build relationships with other mentors and mentees across the university.

To become a mentor, you need to be a current student at the University of Wolverhampton who is in at least your second year of study. Mentors are selected based on their commitment to helping others, their academic achievements, and their willingness to provide guidance and support.

How to Sign Up

Signing up for the Peer Mentorship Program is easy! Follow these steps:

  1. Visit the Peer Mentorship Portal: Go to the Peer Mentorship page on the university website or app.
  2. Fill Out the Application Form: Complete the application form with your personal information, academic interests, and goals for mentorship.
  3. Submit Your Application: Once your form is completed, submit it to join the program.
  4. Get Matched: You will be notified of your mentor or mentee match via email and/or the app.
  5. Start Meeting: Arrange your first meeting with your mentor or mentee. Make sure to communicate your expectations and discuss the best ways to stay in touch.

Mentor and mentee sign-ups are open at the beginning of each semester. Be sure to sign up early to ensure you get paired with the best match for your needs.
